Problem Solving

One approach to: How to solve a problem?

  1. Understand the problem:
  1. What are they asking me to do?
  2. Do I need any information from an user to get started?
  1. Does the information belong to a particular type or is the information coming in a particular format, number, array, dictionary, json, xml, file?
  2. Are there any assumptions about the incoming information?
  1. What do I need to answer as the result or output?
  1. Is the answer a number? Is it a yes or no? Is it a sentence?
  1. How to calculate the required outputs? (use operation box to do that)
  1. Can I do this without math? If not, is there a math operation that helps me with this?
  1. Fill out the chart with the information provided on the “paragraphs” (worksheet).

Data/Information

(Inputs)

Process/Operations/Calculations

Results

(Outputs)

  1. Write down the “recipe” to solve the problem.  (do this while thinking about 1.d)
  1. Use several examples to test your “recipe”
  1. If at least ONE does not work: Ask yourself why and rewrite your “recipe” (Do #3 again).
  2. If they work, proceed to #4.
  1. Start coding // or pseudo coding.
  1. Test it with your examples, if at least one failed go back to #3 and rewrite your “recipe”.

Note: sometimes steps 3 and 4 can be done at the same time.
